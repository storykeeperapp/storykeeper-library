Building Your Audience From Zero  ·  A Personal Roadmap

First, A Mindset Shift

You don't need to become an influencer. You need to be yourself, building something people want. The most engaging content on BookTok right now isn't polished — it's real people sharing genuine passion for books and creativity. That is your biggest advantage.

You have something most creators don't: a real product with a real story behind it. You identified a problem every book lover has, and you built a solution. Just show up and share it honestly — the audience will come.

The "Build in Public" approach works. Sharing your journey — the wins, the setbacks, the late nights — is some of the most watched content on TikTok right now. You don't need a finished product to start. You just need to start.

Simple Rules for Posting

🎥
No face required

Screen recordings with a voiceover work just as well. Start there if you're not comfortable on camera yet.

☀️
Natural light

If you do show your face, sit near a window. It makes a bigger difference than any camera or lighting equipment.

⏱️
Under 60 seconds

Shorter videos get more replays which tells the algorithm it's good content. Build up to longer videos later.

📝
Always add captions

Most people watch without sound. TikTok auto-generates captions — turn them on and proofread them.

🕐
Best posting times

7–9pm weekdays. Weekends are hit or miss. Post consistently rather than perfectly timed.

🚫
Don't delete posts

Low-view posts can pick up later. Deleting signals to the algorithm that your content underperforms.

💬
Engage daily

Spend 15 minutes a day leaving genuine comments on BookTok videos. People click profiles of commenters they like.

Reaching Out to Book Creators

Wait until your beta is stable (roughly 6–8 weeks in) before reaching out to creators. Target small to mid-size accounts (5k–100k followers) first — they respond more often and their audiences are more engaged than mega-influencers.

Before you DM anyone: Follow them for at least a week first. Leave a few genuine comments on their videos. Cold DMs from strangers get ignored — warm DMs from a familiar name get opened.

Realistic Expectations

Building a following from zero takes time. Here's what to expect so you're not discouraged:

TimeframeRealistic Follower GoalWhat This Feels Like
Week 1–210–50 followersSlow. Normal. Keep going.
Month 150–200 followersOne post might do better than expected. Celebrate it.
Month 2200–600 followersStarting to feel real. Comments become conversations.
Month 3500–1500 followersCreator posts go live. Traffic picks up. Launch time.
The creators who succeed aren't the most talented — they're the most consistent. Your first 20 videos will probably get low views. That is completely normal and expected. Keep posting. The algorithm needs data before it knows who to show your content to.
